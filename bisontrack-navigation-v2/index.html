Navigation Redesign — v2 · CRO-Optimised

Language Riders
Actually Use

Hover the nav above or tap the hamburger (mobile). V2 adds real product images, rider-specific copy, and category icons — all built to close the gap between "browsing" and "buying."

Copy: "Style" is Gone

Style → Pit Wear

Every rider knows "Pit Wear" — it's what you wear in the paddock. "Style" means nothing to them and signals t-shirts, not the custom team jerseys and crew shirts that are actually there.

Copy: RTW Suits Signal Availability

Suits → In-Stock Suits · Ships Now

The core RTW differentiator vs. Custom is no wait time. "In-Stock" + green "Ships Now" badge communicates that immediately, removing a buyer's main hesitation before they even land on the page.

Product Images + Prices in Nav

Featured product cards (real CDN images + prices) inside the Suits columns. Users see what they're clicking toward before they click — pre-qualifies intent, reduces bounce on collection pages.

Icons Aid Scannability

Small lime icon tiles on each column header let mobile users parse the category in under a second — faster than reading the label. Especially valuable at mobile where users are scrolling quickly.

"Track Gear" Replaces "Gear"

Gear → Track Gear

Adds context — these are racing-specific items. A rider browsing casually now knows this column is about on-track safety, not general accessories.

"Gloves" → "Racing Gloves"

Gloves → Racing Gloves

Adds specificity — riders searching for track gloves know these are the right ones. Reduces clicks from people looking for winter gloves, improving click quality and time-on-page.
