Funnel Analysis

Leak 1: View Item → Add to Cart

Priority #1 22,166 people view products but only 1,189 add to cart. That's a 5.4% View→ATC rate vs industry standard of 8–12%.

Root Causes Identified

  1. Loox star ratings hidden via CSS — UGC videos and customer photos exist on PDPs, but Loox star ratings and written reviews remain hidden (display: none). UGC builds trust; verified star ratings close the sale for $259–$500 products.
  2. No "Notify Me" for out-of-stock variants — When specific component variants sell out (e.g., popular bezel/dial combos), there's no email capture or waitlist. Demand is lost silently.
  3. Price without value justification — $421 displayed without explaining what's included, what you'd pay separately, or why it's worth it.
  4. No configurator preview — Customers choose components but can't see the finished watch. Survey data confirms this request.
  5. Missing sticky ATC on mobile — 66% of traffic is mobile, but no sticky add-to-cart button means users must scroll back up.
  6. No urgency beyond "low stock" — The static scarcity message doesn't feel genuine.
TheModWatches CRO Audit — March 2026 cazyweb

Funnel Analysis

Leak 2 & 3: Cart → Purchase

Leak 2: ATC → Begin Checkout

65.3% drop 777 of 1,189 ATC events don't proceed to checkout.

  • No cart abandonment incentive — No exit-intent popup or "complete your build" reminder
  • Shipping uncertainty — "Free worldwide shipping" not reinforced at cart
  • Product recommendations have 0% conversion — PDP recommendations generate clicks (12% rate) but zero completed checkouts across 90 days. Engine may be surfacing low-relevance products.

Leak 3: Checkout → Purchase

72.6% drop 299 of 412 who start checkout don't complete.

  • International shipping concerns — "Taxes and duties included" may not be clear enough
  • No express checkout options above the fold (Apple Pay, Shop Pay, Google Pay)
  • Missing reassurance at checkout — No "30-day guarantee" badge during checkout
  • Form length/complexity — Standard Shopify checkout can feel heavy for first-time buyers

Copy & Messaging

Critical Language Constraint & Three Trust Guarantees

Critical Language Constraint

"German-Curated" — NOT "German-made" or "Swiss-made"

Simon explicitly notes: "We are not swiss-made. We are not made in Germany. So let's say 'German-Curated' because our founder is from Germany and he curated the kits himself."

All copy must use "German-Curated" to remain truthful. The Peter Hermann name and heritage are authentic; the manufacturing origin claim is not.

Acceptable: "German-Curated," "Designed in Germany," "German heritage"

Not acceptable: "German-made," "Made in Germany," "Swiss-made," "Swiss-grade"

Three Trust Guarantees (Currently Buried)

These exist but are barely visible. Must be on every PDP, in cart, and in email flows:

1. 30-Day Satisfaction Guarantee

"Satisfied or refunded. Though once you feel the weight of submarine-grade steel, you'll understand why we're confident."

2. 48-Month Precision Warranty

"Your watch parts are covered against manufacturing defects. We'll repair or replace faulty parts. Because quality matters."

3. Simon's Zero-Failure Promise

"Can't get your watch ticking? Send us the parts. We'll assemble them professionally and return your finished watch. (Never happened in our entire history.)"

CRO Impact: These guarantees are the #1 tool for objection handling. The Zero-Failure Promise alone could improve View→ATC by removing the "what if I can't do it?" barrier.

Brand Architecture & Success Tests

Brand Architecture: Master + Sub-brand

Maison Alcée-style brand architecture:

Peter Hermann = Master Brand

The umbrella. Named after Simon's two real grandfathers — authentic heritage, not manufactured. Works across product categories: watches now, knives next, other craft experiences in future.

UWE = "Unfinished Watch Experience"

The watch product sub-brand. Named after Uwe, Simon's friend who passed at 40 — the genuine emotional story that drives the brand.

Letter-Named Models

Future naming: "The U" (for Uwe), "The M", "The H", "The P". Site must support /collections/uwe → "The UWE Collection" with sub-collections by model letter.

Three Success Tests (from Designer Brief)

Simon's explicit validation criteria for the rebrand:

1. The Mirror Test (Personal Validation)

Simon looks at the watch and thinks "this looks f***ing sick." He would wear it proudly. The dial branding makes him proud. Every PDP must create this visceral reaction.

2. The Customer Test (Market Validation)

95% of past customers should say "hell yeah" when asked "would you want this on your dial?" Run the customer test BEFORE full rebrand launch. Use email list to validate.

3. The Price Test (Financial Validation)

$600–$1,000 feels appropriate for the experience. Customers see it as accessible luxury ("I deserve this"), not expensive DIY. The website must justify this price through premium presentation.

Dial Branding, Post-Build Value & CRO Recommendations

Dial Branding: Most Urgent Design Priority

The brief calls empty watch dials the #1 design problem: "Empty dial is killing premium feel. Current M logo is too basic."

  • Dial appearance is the first thing customers evaluate on a PDP
  • Empty dials make the product look "unfinished" negatively (vs aspirational "Unfinished Watch Experience")
  • Competitor DIYwatch.club already has branded dials
  • Once new dial branding exists, update ALL product photography immediately

Post-Build Experience as Value Justification

  • Peter Hermann collector's seal engraved on components
  • Certificate of craftsmanship with collector's seal
  • Serial numbers on each build (adds exclusivity)
  • Limited edition markers for special releases
  • Watches designed to be "worn and talked about — the trophy, the conversation piece"
CRO implication: Create a "What You Receive" section on PDPs showing the complete experience — not just kit components, but certificate, seal, serial number. Transforms purchase from "buying parts" to "joining a legacy."

CRO Recommendations Aligned with Rebrand

  1. Reviews and social proof: Curate premium-feeling testimonials. Show "CEO," "Architect," "Surgeon" in reviewer titles.
  2. Photography upgrade: Editorial lifestyle photography. Watches on wrists at dinner, boardrooms, outdoors.
  3. Gift positioning: Frame as "an experience, not a product." The gift is "3 hours of craftsmanship that results in a timepiece."
  4. Price anchoring: Stop leading with discounts. Lead with value and heritage.
  5. Community framing: "A community of makers" with curated, premium-presenting UGC.
  6. Founder story: Make three-generation German heritage central. The story is REAL — handle with authenticity.
  7. Copy language shift: Replace "DIY" and "kit." Use: "maker experience," "premium timepiece," "Handgemacht."
  8. Unboxing as selling point: Feature 8-layer premium unboxing on PDPs, homepage, and ads.
